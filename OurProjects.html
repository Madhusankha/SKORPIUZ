IMPLEMENTING NON-DESTRUCTIVE TEST TO DETECT PINHOLES IN DISPOSABLE GLOVES

MECHANICAL AND MANUFACTURING

Wayamba University of Sri Lanka were invited to attend a university collaborative projects program at MIDAS Safety at Biyagama. The problem was to build a method to detect pinholes in disposable gloves.  It was mandatory to find pinholes in the gloves to increase their product quality. After following various testing procedures. Finally, we came up with a viable engineering solution to detect pinholes in disposable gloves after their manufacturing process. The company was satisfied with our solution method. We were about to begin the project but unfortunately, the company conclude the project due to the pandemic situation. DESIGNING A DIGITAL CLOCK

ELECTRICAL AND ELECTRONIC

A digital clock is a type of clock that displays the time digitally.  The times obtained by analog clocks come from a pendulum or a spring. Today, most of the digital clocks are made using microcontrollers. And also, those are more accurate than analog clocks. The first digital pocket watch was the invention of Austrian engineer Josef Pallweber in 1883 and he used mechanism called “Jump-Hours” to counting the time. However, by today digital clock came with lot of multifunction features. In this project the clock to be designed is something like HH: MM: SS, which represents hours, minutes and seconds respectively. This circuit implemented using Proteus software.
